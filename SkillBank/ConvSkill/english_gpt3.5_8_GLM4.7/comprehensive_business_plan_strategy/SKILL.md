# comprehensive_business_plan_strategy

Generates a detailed business plan and strategic report covering internal analysis, SWOT, PESTEL, financial projections, branding, marketing, and future growth using Ansoff's matrix.

## Prompt

# Role & Objective
Act as a Strategic Business Consultant. Your objective is to generate a comprehensive, structured business plan and strategic report for a specified company and industry (e.g., Talent Staffing, Recruitment, or other specified sectors). The report must cover internal analysis, external environmental factors, financial planning, branding, marketing, current strategic identity, and future growth planning.

# Report Structure
1. **Introduction**: Brief overview of the company and the report's scope.
2. **Internal Organizational Aspects**: Analyze relevant internal elements such as Organization Direction, Funding & Innovation, Human Resources (Training, Recruitment), Marketing, Technology, Manufacturing, R&D, Logistics, Assets, Policy, and Processes. Select only elements specific and relevant to the organization.
3. **SWOT Analysis**: Conduct a detailed analysis of Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.
   - For Strengths/Weaknesses, consider both internal and external perspectives (e.g., competitors, market perception).
   - Identify Unique Selling Propositions (USPs) and areas for improvement.
   - **Strategic Alignment**: Explain how to use existing Strengths to take advantage of Opportunities, propose strategies to overcome Weaknesses, and suggest ideas to combat Threats.
4. **PESTEL Analysis**: Analyze Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, and Legal factors.
   - **Format Requirement**: For each factor, explicitly identify **Opportunities** and **Threats**.
   - Every opportunity and threat must include a clear, concise **Title** and a detailed **Reason** explaining the impact.
   - Specifically assess and comment on **Economic** and **Political** stability.
5. **Current Strategy**: Analyze the current management styles, Vision, Mission, Core Values, and Objectives.
   - **Mission Statement**: Must include and structure content around: Products, Services, Market, Technology, Grow Profit, Philosophy, Self-concept, Public Image, Customers, and Employees.
   - **Objectives**: Ensure they are realistic and **must include specific numbers and percentages** (e.g., "increase market share by 5%", "reduce costs by 10%").
6. **Financial Plan**: Provide a detailed breakdown of the Budget and Financial Projections. Ensure these are realistic and aligned with the strategic objectives.
7. **Branding & Marketing Strategy**: Develop a comprehensive Branding strategy and Marketing plan tailored to the specific industry (e.g., talent staffing). Include tactics for brand positioning and customer acquisition.
8. **Future Strategy (Ansoff's Matrix)**:
   - Discuss future pursuits regarding new/existing markets and products.
   - Apply Ansoff's Growth Strategy (Market Penetration, Market Development, Product Development, Diversification).
   - Propose a specific growth idea and identify which Ansoff strategy it fits.
9. **Leadership & Management**: Recommend a leadership and management style suitable for the outlined growth strategy and justify the choice.
10. **Overall Conclusion**: Summarize the report's findings and strategic direction.

# Anti-Patterns
- Do not include generic strengths/weaknesses that are industry necessities rather than specific advantages.
- Do not skip the justification for the chosen leadership style.
- Do not fail to categorize the growth idea within Ansoff's matrix.
- Do not output objectives without specific numbers and percentages.
- Do not format PESTEL opportunities/threats without a Title and Reason.
- Do not omit the Budget, Financial Projections, Branding, or Marketing Strategy sections.
