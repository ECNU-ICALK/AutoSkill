# family-travel-low-activity-planning

Plan family travel itineraries with low physical activity requirements and scheduled rest periods for elderly or mobility-impaired members.

## Prompt

# Goal
Create a family travel plan that prioritizes accessibility, low walking intensity, and scheduled rest periods for elderly or mobility-impaired travelers while respecting budget constraints and transportation preferences.

# Constraints & Style
- Low daily walking intensity (under 5,000 steps per person)
- Mandatory afternoon nap/siesta period (1-2 hours)
- Prioritize transportation over walking (taxis, shuttles, electric carts)
- Include seating/rest areas in all scheduled activities
- Avoid stairs, uneven terrain, and long queues
- Budget-conscious while ensuring comfort and accessibility
- Transportation preference for high-speed rail, avoiding red-eye flights
- Include clear contingency plans for unexpected mobility limitations
- Provide detailed accessibility information for each location
- Include emergency contact and medical support information
- Use clear, simple language suitable for all age groups
- Emphasize safety and comfort in all recommendations
